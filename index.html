The 30-Second Read

  1. Feed first. If Feed is not live or freshness is stale, do not trust the signal yet.
  2. Action State second. This tells you whether the market is asleep, forming, active, or messy.
  3. Bias third. Read LONG or SHORT in the Action State line and What If Now.
  4. Target Scores fourth. These show the possible distance and time runway.
  5. Clock Lens fifth. Check whether the current MYT block supports this type of extraction.
  6. Price-Time Array Lens sixth. Check whether price is near one of your automatic horizontal arrays and a vertical time window.
  7. Entry Lens last. This is the timing gate. ENTER, WAIT, or AVOID.

Action State

WAITNothing strong enough yet. Watch, but do not force a trade.
PRE-FORMATIONEarly shape is appearing. Prepare, but still wait for confirmation.
FORMATIONThe setup is serious. Start checking Entry Lens carefully.
ACTUAL STATEThe model trigger is active. You still need Entry Lens and risk filters to agree.
RESIDUAL / CHOPMarket is messy or noisy. Usually avoid new entries.

Entry Lens

1m PulseImmediate push. Is price moving in the intended direction right now?
4m IgnitionShort burst. Is momentum beginning to catch?
13m EntryMain lower-time-frame entry window. This is the execution pane.
39m ConfirmBigger confirmation. Helps avoid entering into chop or a fake push.

Target Scores In Plain English

Each row asks: "How likely is this size move within this time window?"

350 / 39mA faster scalp-style move of 350 points within 39 minutes.
500 / 245mA bigger intraday runway of 500 points within 245 minutes.
750 / 245mA larger move. Stronger potential, but usually needs broader alignment.
Score numberReadiness. Low means weak. Near trigger means forming. Over trigger means active.

For 1000PEPEUSDT, 1 point equals 0.0000001. A 500-point move equals 0.0000500.

24h Clock Lens

This tells you whether the current MYT clock block historically suits quick extraction, core trades, runners, or caution.

20:00-21:59Prime extraction block. Best for 350-700 points across 39-245 minutes.
02:00-03:59Quality window. Fewer signals, often cleaner when Entry Lens agrees.
06:00-11:59Runner-friendly. Better for 700-1100 point continuation ideas.
12:00-13:59Selective 350-500 point fast/core trades.
18:00-19:59Build-up block before the strongest 20:00 window.
14:00-15:59Caution block. Demand cleaner confirmation or stand aside.
22:00-23:59Noisier block. Many signals can appear, but chasing risk is higher.

Price-Time Array Lens

This replaces your daily manual horizontal and vertical line drawing inside JESUS.

Nearest ArrayThe closest automatic horizontal line from your fixed MYT candle-low anchors.
DistanceHow many points live price is above or below that line. Small distance means price is interacting with the array.
Vertical WindowYour fixed time stamps. If a reclaim or rejection happens near one, the reaction deserves more attention.
TOUCHPrice is on the line. Do not enter from touch alone. Wait for reclaim, rejection, or Entry Lens agreement.
RECLAIMPrice swept below the array and got back above it. Long idea only if JESUS bias and Entry Lens agree.
REJECT / BROKENPrice failed at the array or broke below it. Short idea only if JESUS bias and Entry Lens agree.
FARNo array edge nearby. Read the normal dashboard first.

Support Panels

What If NowThe plain-language trade-now bias. Use it as a quick sanity check.
RunwayThe strongest target and its time window. This tells you how much room JESUS sees.
Lead TimeWhether the move is active now, forming soon, or not present yet.

Risk Filters

Path GeometryHow clean or choppy the path is. High chop means entries are more dangerous.
Flow PressureBuy/sell pressure and activity. You want flow to support your direction.
Anti-ThesisContradictions. If this looks ugly, reduce trust in the setup.

Simple Decision Rules

Consider ENTERAction State is FORMATION or ACTUAL, Entry Lens says ENTER, direction is clear, Target Scores show runway, Clock Lens fits the target tier, Price-Time Array does not conflict, and no major chop warning appears.
WAITPRE-FORMATION appears, Target Scores are rising, Entry Lens has only partial agreement, or Price-Time Array says TOUCH. Let the setup mature.
AVOIDFeed is stale, Action State says RESIDUAL / CHOP, Entry Lens says AVOID, bias is thin, Price-Time Array conflicts with direction, or the panes disagree.

Common Scenarios

Scenario 1: Clean LONG Entry

Action State is FORMATION or ACTUAL. Bias says LONG. Target Scores are strong. Entry Lens says ENTER. Flow supports LONG and Price-Time Array is FAR, ABOVE, or RECLAIM.

Layman read: The runway exists, the direction is clear, and the entry timing agrees.

Scenario 2: Clean SHORT Entry

Action State is FORMATION or ACTUAL. Bias says SHORT. Target Scores are strong. Entry Lens says ENTER. Flow supports SHORT and Price-Time Array is FAR, REJECT, or BROKEN.

Layman read: Same as a long setup, but the pressure points downward.

Scenario 3: Early Setup, Not Ready

Action State is PRE-FORMATION. Target Scores are rising, but Entry Lens says WAIT.

Layman read: Something may be building, but the lower time frames have not given permission yet.

Scenario 4: Target Scores Look Good, Entry Lens Says AVOID

Runway exists, but the 1m, 4m, 13m, or 39m panes disagree, flow fights the direction, or Price-Time Array conflicts with the bias.

Layman read: There may be distance available, but the entry timing is poor. Do not chase.

Scenario 5: RESIDUAL / CHOP

Action State says RESIDUAL / CHOP, even if price is moving.

Layman read: The market is noisy. You can be right on direction and still get shaken out.

Scenario 6: Bias Flips Quickly

Bias changes from LONG to SHORT or SHORT to LONG while Target Scores stay moderate.

Layman read: The market is undecided. Wait for one side to dominate.

Scenario 7: Feed Is Not Live

Feed is reconnecting, stale, unavailable, or the price stops updating.

Layman read: You are reading old information. Pause until the feed is live again.

Best Practical Rule

Do not enter just because one panel looks exciting. JESUS is strongest when Action State, bias, Target Scores, Clock Lens, Price-Time Array Lens, Entry Lens, and risk filters point in the same direction.

Quick Checklist Before Clicking Buy or Sell

  1. Is Feed live and fresh?
  2. Is Action State better than WAIT?
  3. Is the LONG or SHORT bias obvious?
  4. Do Target Scores show enough runway for the trade?
  5. Does Clock Lens fit the target tier, or is it warning you to be stricter?
  6. Is Price-Time Array Lens supportive, neutral, or telling you to wait at a touch?
  7. Does Entry Lens say ENTER, or at least stop saying AVOID?
  8. Are chop, contradiction, and flow acceptable?
  9. Do you already know your invalidation point before entering?
